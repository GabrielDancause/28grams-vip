Frequently Asked Questions

What food has the highest protein per calorie?
Based on our data, supplements like Collagen Peptides and Whey Protein Isolate have the highest protein per calorie. Among whole foods, Egg Whites, Chicken Breast, and Turkey Breast rank the highest.
How do you calculate protein per calorie?
Protein per calorie is calculated by dividing the total grams of protein by the total calories. For example, if a food has 31g of protein and 165 calories, the ratio is 31 / 165 = 0.1879g of protein per calorie.
What is a good protein per calorie ratio?
A ratio above 0.10g per calorie is generally considered excellent for lean protein sources. This means for every 10 calories, you get at least 1 gram of protein.
Are plant-based proteins efficient?
Some plant-based proteins like Seitan, Tofu, and Edamame have decent protein-to-calorie ratios. However, many plant sources like nuts or beans contain higher amounts of fats or carbs, which lowers their pure protein efficiency compared to lean meats.
Why does protein per calorie matter?
For people tracking macros, especially those in a caloric deficit, maximizing protein intake while keeping calories low is essential for muscle retention, satiety, and meeting fitness goals.

Methodology

Data was collected utilizing OpenFoodFacts databases and USDA nutritional guidelines. All macro values are standardized per 100g serving size. Protein per calorie is determined by dividing total protein (g) by total calories (kcal).

While some whole foods naturally vary, these values represent the average nutritional profile. Supplements (like protein powders) are included for reference but naturally score highest due to being isolated nutrients.
